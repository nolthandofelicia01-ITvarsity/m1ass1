Rio de Janeiro

Rio de Janeiro is one of the most visited cities in the Southern Hemisphere and is known for its natural settings.Rio is recognized as one of the world's most beautiful and interesting urban centres. Although Rio continues to be the preeminent icon of Brazil in the eyes of many in the world, in reality its location, architecture, inhabitants and lifestyle make it highly unique when compared with other Brazilian cities, especially the country's capital of Brasilis or the much larger city of Sao Paul. In Rio street arts is legal. Painters are free to express themselves on the streets and you are sure to find some interesting graphics on most walls. Brazil's most oldest and famous beer is brewed in Rio called the Bramha. For beer lovers this is a must have to quench your thirst while touring the city. This place also has beautiful beaches. It is abundantly blessed with natural attractions. There are so many sights that Rio has to offer, Christ the Redeemer Statue is an iconic statue atop Mount Corcovado with a granite peak with cable cars to its summit. The city is also known for its sprawling favelas (shanty towns). Its raucous Carnival festival, featuring parades floats, flamboyant costumes and samba dancers, is considered the world's largest. We also have Chic boutiques and Ipanema Beach. Copacabana art deco Palace and beach life as well, Escadaria Selaron is an iconic stairway covered with mosaic tile. There is so much about this city that is fascinating, its an eye catcher.
